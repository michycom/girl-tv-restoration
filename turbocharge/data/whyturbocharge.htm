Why Turbocharge ?


What are the benefits of turbocharging ?

A turbocharged diesel engine far out performs its naturally aspirated counterpart. By using a turbo to increase the volume of air and fuel, a greater power output is achieved with a lot less work and at a lower cost. With an overall increase of engine output of some 25%, a job which previously could not be performed by a naturally aspirated engine can be easily carried out.

Is my engine safe to turbocharge ?

Over the years, engine manufacturers have designed and devolped a range of diesel engines with a very high specification component build. In today's modern diesel engines there is very little internal difference between the various applications of power supplied by the manufacturers.

What are the operator's benefits ?

If you are looking for a higher horsepowered, light-weight tractor with increased PTO output, look no further. With the turbocharger installed you not only benefit immediately but you can save thousands of pounds. The equipment can operate under conditions which previously required a heavier and more expensive tractor, and the operator also benefits from being able to move up into a higher gear.

What about the environment ?

With a view to satisfying present and future European emission regulations, compared with naturally aspirated diesel engines our conversions have been designed to reduce significantly both exhaust emissions and unburned hydrocarbons.
